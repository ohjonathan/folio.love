## Canonical verdict

**Revise and re-review.**

## Rationale

Consolidation rule applied (playbook.md P5 evidence-weighted consensus, adapted
to Pre-A): a blocker-severity finding by one reviewing family, supported by
direct citation of the proposal doc's own contradictions and by codebase
cross-references, overrides an opposing family's Approve verdict when the
opposing family did not engage with those specific findings.

Reviewer 2 (gemini, Alignment+Technical) identified zero blockers and gave a
clean Proceed. Reviewer 1 (claude-sub, Adversarial+Product) identified three
blocker-severity findings with direct-citation evidence. All three are facts
about the proposal doc itself, not judgment calls:

- **PR-A-1 (blocker, claude-sub):** Internal numeric contradiction between
  §10.1 ("exact rejected-suggestion resurfacing without material input change
  is a product defect, not harmless repetition") and §13.1 gate 4 (same event
  "stays at or below 5%"). Zero-tolerance and 5%-tolerance cannot both be
  the contract. Reviewer 2 did not surface this. Evidence is direct
  quotation; the contradiction is present in the proposal doc regardless of
  posture.
- **PR-A-2 (blocker, claude-sub):** `§9` caps "20 new proposals per
  engagement per run by default" but neither "engagement" nor "run" is
  defined in terms the current code can persist. `folio/links.py` has no
  run-counter or engagement-segmentation. A Phase A spec author would either
  invent infrastructure that does not exist (risking scope-lock violations)
  or silently reinterpret the cap as per-page rather than per-production
  (shipping a weaker guarantee than the proposal claims). Reviewer 2 treated
  queue bounds as operational guardrails without examining operationalization.
- **PR-A-3 (blocker, claude-sub):** `input_fingerprint`'s "normalized claim
  identity" is undefined in §7. Without a canonical normalization function
  two producers can disagree on whether a repeated suggestion is "the same"
  proposal — defeating rejection memory (§10.1), which is the whole point of
  the spec. Reviewer 2 endorsed fingerprint rules without flagging the
  undefined term.

None of these are judgment calls. They are facts in the proposal doc that a
Phase A spec author would have to resolve silently if left unaddressed, and
any B.1/B.2 reviewer (especially an adversarial one) would catch the
resulting inconsistency at spec-review time. Moving forward without revision
just shifts the blockers downstream one phase.

Product-lens findings (claude-sub PR-P-1 through PR-P-8) add non-blocker but
meaningful scope/scoping concerns — notably PR-P-6 (scope leakage: §13.1
gate 6 on diagram-archetype clustering contradicts the rev-2 narrowing
claim) and PR-P-3 (acceptance criteria in §14 are doc-compliance checkboxes
not user-value criteria). These strengthen the Revise path.

## Contradictions between reviewers

No factual contradictions between the two reviewers' findings. The verdict
disagreement is explained by posture coverage: gemini's Alignment+Technical
lens checked consistency with approved reference docs (and found none
violated), while claude-sub's Adversarial+Product lens checked the
proposal's internal consistency and operationalization (and found three
blocker-level gaps). Both reviews are correct within their posture; the
proposal is simultaneously "aligned with external reference docs" and
"internally self-contradictory in three places." Pre-A.proposal's 2-lens
setup is specifically designed to surface this kind of split — it is
working as intended here.

The lifecycle **does not advance to Phase A** on this run. Under the
operator brief's halt list, any Pre-A verdict ≠ "Proceed to Phase A"
halts the session. The Revise verdict is preserved.
