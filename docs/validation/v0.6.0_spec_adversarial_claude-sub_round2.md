## Verdict justification

Round 2 scope was narrow: verify closure of X-1, X-2, SF-1..SF-8, and hunt for new attack surfaces in v1.1 additions.

- **X-1 and X-2 are closed cleanly.** The 7-caller inventory is verified by fresh `rg` run; the tuple-key prose/pseudo-code contradiction is fully resolved with §2 deferring to §4.2.
- **7 of 8 should-fix items closed.** SF-5's commitment ambiguity from Round 1 (author was offered two options: add flag OR add OQ entry) was resolved by the author doing **neither**. The operator-debug gap remains.
- **One new blocker (X-R2-1)** and **two new minors** surface on v1.1 additions. X-R2-1 is an internal contradiction between §11 and §3 of the same spec; a Phase C implementer reading §11 reaches a false conclusion about the mechanism, even though the actual mechanism (via `_build_rejection_key`) works correctly. The spec has a falsifiable correctness claim embedded in §11's carry-forward disposition, and that claim is false under the spec's own definitions. This is a blocker by the same standard that Round 1 applied to the "only two callers" factual error.
- **Halt condition not met.** I was able to construct findings on the v1.1 additions on the first adversarial pass; the halt check (two passes with no finding) is inactive.

Spec is closer to Approve but not there. One ≤15-minute author edit closes X-R2-1 (rewrite §11 PR-A-14 row to drop the false "preserved in `basis_fingerprint`" claim and replace with "preserved separately in the `_build_rejection_key` tuple's source_id/target_id fields"). SF-5 closure requires either (a) adding the `--show-suppressed` flag to §4.4 (adds CLI surface and one test), or (b) recording SF-5 explicitly in §5 Open Questions / §11 carry-forward as a committed follow-up with a named future-slice owner. Option (b) is lower-effort.

MN-R2-1 and MN-R2-2 are trivial copy-edits; not blocking.
