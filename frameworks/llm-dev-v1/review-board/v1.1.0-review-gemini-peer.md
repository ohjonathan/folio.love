# Peer Review — frameworks-llm-dev-v1 / v1.1.0-review / gemini

Lens: peer (quality / completeness / implementability / template fidelity).
Scope: Gemini family's v1.1 bundle review, templates + walkthrough slice. Focus on template frontmatter patterns, dogfood coverage, and handoff clarity.

## 1. Completeness check
**Template 19 (Product) structural twin:** Template 19 does not strictly have the same sections as Template 03 (it replaces "Diagram-prose cross-reference" with "Product-surface cross-reference"), but it retains the correct review-board shape: Context header, specific evaluation sections, Issues found, Positive observations, Verdict, Notes. The frontmatter shape perfectly matches the other review-board templates (`id`, `version`, `role`, `audience`, `wraps`, `lens`, `required_tokens`, `depends_on`). The `BEGIN PRODUCT REVIEW` and `END PRODUCT REVIEW` markers are present. The mandatory stance paragraph from playbook §13.4 is included verbatim.

**Templates 16, 17, 18 frontmatter patterns:** All three successfully follow the `wraps: 01-worker-session-contract.md` idiom. Every single token referenced in their bodies (e.g., `<PROPOSAL_DOC_PATH>`, `<TRIAGE_INPUT_PATH>`, `<VALIDATION_RUN_INPUT_PATH>`) is accurately declared in the `required_tokens` and `optional_tokens` frontmatter blocks.

**Walkthrough dogfood coverage:** `examples/walkthrough-user-facing.md` successfully dogfoods Template 16 (Pre-A.proposal), Template 19 (B.1/B.2/D.2), and Template 06 with the 4-row intake (B.3/D.3). It explicitly demonstrates the P10 same-family-separate-session pattern. However, Templates 17 (Triage) and 18 (Validation Run) are not dogfooded in the walkthrough; they are only mentioned as alternative Pre-A choices.

**v1.0.0 backward-compat witness:** `examples/walkthrough-mini-deliverable.md` remains byte-accurate to the v1.0.0 version.

**Manifest example:** `manifest/example-user-facing-manifest.yaml` clearly demonstrates the same-family-separate-session pattern. In Phase B.1 and B.2, `codex` is assigned as `adversarial` in one block, and as `product` in a separate block.
