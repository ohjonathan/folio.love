## 1. Product Framing & Problem Statement

The revision does not begin from a user problem. It begins from an architectural worry. Read the spec's Overview and Goals and count the user-facing sentences: zero. The Overview ([`tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md:21-35`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/specs/tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md#L21)) says the purpose is "to let Folio discover patterns across messy consulting corpora without allowing probabilistic guesses to become ontology or canonical graph truth by default." That is a stance, not a problem. The user, who is one person (Johnny) preparing for McKinsey engagements, never appears. There is no moment of pain, no rejected workflow, no "today this breaks when X happens." The PRD's strategic memo at [`strategic_direction_memo.md:17`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/product/strategic_direction_memo.md#L17) is explicit that output must be usable in live engagements; the revised Tier 4 proposal does not name a single engagement moment this improves.

Contrast with the original handoff brief at [`Folio_Feature_Handoff_Brief.md:41`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/product/Folio_Feature_Handoff_Brief.md#L41), which frames features as "Johnny currently takes meeting notes in OneNote, records audio, transcribes, then manually asks ChatGPT to summarize. Five manual steps per meeting." That's a product problem. This revision has nothing comparable. There is no "before this PR" narrative showing what is broken for Johnny today that the latent/proposal/canonical boundary fixes.

The closest thing to a who-benefits claim is that FR-810 helps with "body-coverage blockers visible before graph-density work is attempted" ([`02_Product_Requirements_Document.md:1124`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/product/02_Product_Requirements_Document.md#L1124)). But "graph density" is not a user goal. Johnny doesn't care about graph density. He cares about SteerCo prep, finding the right interview quote, drafting a deliverable without re-reading 30 decks. Nowhere does the revision connect FR-810 to any of those outcomes. The same applies to FR-811 (trust-aware surfacing), FR-812 (relation-schema validation), FR-813 (latent discovery layer), and FR-814 (proposal lifecycle): every one is justified in terms of internal system properties (trust, validation, lifecycle, non-canonical). None carry a "so that Johnny can..." clause.

By when does this deliver value? The roadmap stretches from Week 23 to Week 34+ ([`04_Implementation_Roadmap.md:500`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/product/04_Implementation_Roadmap.md#L500)). Weeks 26-31 are entirely consumed by graph-quality layer, discovery/proposal foundation, and proposal lifecycle governance — infrastructure weeks before related-links, MOCs, or synthesize are even touched. The only committed user-visible Tier 4 artifact is digest (Weeks 23-25). Everything else has been pushed behind a proposal layer that, by its own admission, has no new user-facing CLI (see Non-Goals table, [`tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md:58`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/specs/tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md#L58)).

This is internally-motivated architecture work with a product-shaped wrapper.

## 2. The Latent → Proposal → Canonical Boundary

Is this boundary load-bearing for the user? Walk through what Johnny actually experiences in his day. He runs `folio digest`. He runs `folio links review`. He runs `folio enrich`. At no point does he inspect a "latent discovery layer," reason about a "proposal object," or distinguish a `suggested` proposal from a `queued` one. The boundary between latent and proposal is entirely machine-internal. And yet the spec commits three new concept names (latent, proposal, canonical), a new three-row table in the ontology doc ([`Folio_Ontology_Architecture.md:128-133`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/architecture/Folio_Ontology_Architecture.md#L128)), and two new PRD requirement IDs (FR-813, FR-814) to formalize it.

Does the user ever notice it in a good way? Yes, in exactly one sense: they notice that their `folio links review` queue does not balloon with the same rejected suggestion on every enrich run. That is the rejection-memory benefit, and it is real. But rejection memory does not require a three-layer terminology. It requires a rejection table keyed by a stable fingerprint, and it could have been bolted onto the existing `folio links` proposal schema in a quarter of the words. The log at [`2026-04-15_tier-4-graph-ops-layer.md:22`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/logs/2026-04-15_tier-4-graph-ops-layer.md#L22) even confirms that proposals already live at `_llm_metadata.<producer>.axes.relationships.proposals` and confirmation already writes canonical state. The "shared proposal contract" is partially there. A three-state nomenclature is not what is missing.

Separation worth the ceremony? The ceremony is: a minimum 9-field contract on every proposal ([`tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md:90-100`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/specs/tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md#L90)), 8 lifecycle states ([`tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md:109-116`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/specs/tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md#L109)), `evidence_bundle`/`reason_bundle`/`trust_bundle`/`schema_gate_result` on every suggestion. This is a significant commitment. Merge candidates don't need evidence bundles in the same way link candidates do. Diagram archetype groupings don't have a natural `source_id`/`target_id` split. Topic clusters don't have "targets" at all. The uniform contract is going to bend under the weight of diverse proposal types, and the spec offers no accommodation for that strain beyond "may support more fields later" ([`tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md:102`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/specs/tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md#L102)).

The architecture doc even hedges the terminology: "In discussion this may look like a 'shadow graph,' but Folio's formal product terms are **latent discovery layer** and **proposal layer**" ([`Folio_Ontology_Architecture.md:150`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/architecture/Folio_Ontology_Architecture.md#L150)). That hedge betrays the problem: the author is naming a concept they themselves are not sure is distinct enough from its predecessor terms to deserve new labels.

The boundary is real for implementers. It is not product shape. It is design-time scaffolding being surfaced as product language.

## 3. Scope Challenge

Given shipped Tier 3 graph-ops — `folio links`, `folio graph`, `folio enrich`, provenance, entity registry (all confirmed shipped in [`2026-04-15_tier-4-graph-ops-layer.md`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/logs/2026-04-15_tier-4-graph-ops-layer.md)) — this proposal is too big and the wrong shape.

Too big: Tier 4 grew from 10+ weeks / 17.5 weeks estimated to 12+ weeks / 22 weeks ([`04_Implementation_Roadmap.md:651`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/product/04_Implementation_Roadmap.md#L651), [`06_Prioritization_Matrix.md:134`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/product/06_Prioritization_Matrix.md#L134)). That's an additional 4.5 weeks of effort for work that ships no new user-visible surface (F-414 and F-415 together total 2 weeks, but are sandwiched inside infrastructure weeks 28-31, pushing related-links, MOCs, and synthesize out by six weeks). The proposal claims "No new CLI command families" in its Non-Goals ([`tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md:58`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/specs/tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md#L58)) and simultaneously adds FR-810 `folio enrich diagnose` as a new command in the same PR ([`02_Product_Requirements_Document.md:1106`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/product/02_Product_Requirements_Document.md#L1106)). That is internally contradictory: the spec says no new CLI; the PRD adds a new CLI.

Wrong shape: the cheapest thing that would deliver the actual value is to take the existing `_llm_metadata.<producer>.axes.relationships.proposals` pattern that already works and layer three behaviors onto it — (a) rejection memory with a staleness fingerprint, (b) a `review_status: flagged` input filter, (c) relation-schema validation when `folio links confirm` writes canonical state. These three behaviors map to FR-810-ish diagnose, FR-811 trust gating, and FR-812 schema validation. They do not require FR-813 or FR-814, they do not require eight lifecycle states, and they do not require an abstract "latent discovery layer" term.

The proposal does not converge toward that cheapest version. It diverges from it. It introduces an entirely new concept layer (latent discovery) that is not yet producing any clusters, similarities, or candidate merges in the codebase (the only proposals shipped today are link and provenance proposals), and names it in PRD-grade SHALL language before a single topic cluster has ever been generated, reviewed, or rejected by a user.

Expand first, codify second. This proposal codifies first.

## 4. User Workflow Risk

Walk the end-to-end flow as a Tier 4 operator on an active engagement.

1. `folio convert deck.pptx` — shipped, works. Evidence note lands with review_status set by extraction signal.
2. `folio ingest transcript.md` — shipped. Interaction note lands.
3. `folio enrich` — shipped. LLM runs, relationships proposed into `_llm_metadata.<producer>.axes.relationships.proposals`. But under FR-810, if the note's managed sections cannot be safely updated, the operator must have run `folio enrich diagnose` first to see why. That is a new precondition the spec does not enforce: it says diagnose is "read-only diagnostic" ([`02_Product_Requirements_Document.md:1111`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/product/02_Product_Requirements_Document.md#L1111)) but does not say `enrich` warns when diagnose findings are non-empty. Silent skip remains possible.
4. `folio links review` — shipped. Now under the proposal revision, this surface must display `trust_bundle`, `evidence_bundle`, `reason_bundle`, `schema_gate_result` per proposal ([`tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md:90-100`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/specs/tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md#L90)). The CLI review UX for these four bundles is nowhere specified. An operator at 10pm doing SteerCo prep does not want four JSON blobs per proposal; they want a one-line reason and a keystroke decision. The revision does not say how bundles render for a human, and that is where user friction will land.
5. `folio digest` — under FR-811, flagged source-backed inputs are excluded by default ([`02_Product_Requirements_Document.md:1137`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/product/02_Product_Requirements_Document.md#L1137), [`tier4_digest_design_spec.md:97`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/specs/tier4_digest_design_spec.md#L97)). But many notes legitimately flag themselves (e.g., `synthesis_requires_review` is added to every digest note at [`tier4_digest_design_spec.md:173`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/specs/tier4_digest_design_spec.md#L173)). This is silent information loss risk. If Johnny flags an interview as "needs re-review" for a minor attribution question, it silently drops out of the daily digest. The digest spec lacks an `--include-flagged` override in v1 ([`tier4_digest_design_spec.md:64`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/specs/tier4_digest_design_spec.md#L64)). There is no safety valve; the operator cannot even temporarily widen the set without editing frontmatter. The Trust Notes section of the digest body is supposed to disclose exclusions ([`tier4_digest_design_spec.md:222`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/specs/tier4_digest_design_spec.md#L222)), but by then the digest has already been written without them — the exclusion disclosure is a footnote, not a gate.
6. `folio synthesize doc_a doc_b` — same flagged-exclusion semantics. Pairwise synthesis where one doc is flagged will either silently skip or error; the PRD does not say which ([`02_Product_Requirements_Document.md:1007-1010`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/product/02_Product_Requirements_Document.md#L1007)).
7. Rejection memory ([`tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md:136-148`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/specs/tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md#L136)): a proposal stays suppressed "until its staleness basis changes materially." What is "materially"? If Johnny rejects a proposed link between `deck_a` and `deck_b`, then reruns enrich, the `input_fingerprint` depends on which bytes feed into it. If the enrich prompt changes (new LLM profile), fingerprint changes, and the rejected proposal resurfaces. The spec does not tell the implementer which inputs count. That is a workflow-breaking ambiguity: either the operator re-sees rejected proposals on every model upgrade (unbounded review burden), or rejection memory is too sticky and real content changes are missed.
8. Stale invalidation triggers include "entity identity changes such as merges" ([`tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md:155`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/specs/tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md#L155)). A person-merge today rewrites internal references ([`2026-04-15_tier-4-graph-ops-layer.md:32`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/logs/2026-04-15_tier-4-graph-ops-layer.md#L32)). So every merge in a 100-person registry invalidates every proposal that ever touched any affected person — review burden explodes exactly when the user does hygiene. The spec has no dampener.

The workflow risk is real. Hidden exclusion, ambiguous staleness, bundle-heavy review UX, and unbounded review burden after hygiene operations.

## 5. Sequencing & Dependency Risk

Are FR-810 / FR-811 / FR-812 the highest-value next step? No. They are premature.

FR-810 (enrich diagnose) is justified by "body-protection blockers are visible before graph-density work is attempted" ([`02_Product_Requirements_Document.md:1124`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/product/02_Product_Requirements_Document.md#L1124)). But graph-density work is not yet a thing users are doing. It is being proposed. The command solves a problem that will exist if and only if a later problem also exists. That is a textbook case of scope-chasing. Ship digest, see whether enrich-protection is actually biting, then add diagnose.

FR-811 (trust-aware graph behavior) is a good instinct, but the default-exclude-flagged rule ([`02_Product_Requirements_Document.md:1137`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/product/02_Product_Requirements_Document.md#L1137)) is a policy decision with no empirical basis in the revision. It assumes flagged content is more harmful than missing content in synthesis. For a consultant who flags "this needs double-checking" on an interview note, silently excluding that note from digest and search may be the opposite of what they want. The spec commits to the policy as SHALL before the tradeoff has been tested.

FR-812 (relation-schema validation) is the most defensible of the three and probably should ship. But governing four relations (`supersedes`, `impacts`, `draws_from`, `depends_on`) while deferring the two most commonly-requested ones (`relates_to`, `instantiates`, see [`02_Product_Requirements_Document.md:1166`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/product/02_Product_Requirements_Document.md#L1166)) is odd; `relates_to` in particular is where most generic LLM-suggested links will land, and it is the one deferred.

Premature fanout: F-414 and F-415 (discovery/proposal foundation and lifecycle governance, [`06_Prioritization_Matrix.md:115-117`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/product/06_Prioritization_Matrix.md#L115)) together take 2 weeks of Johnny's implementation time, before a single cluster or similarity has been generated by the system. The prioritization matrix rates both "High" value / "Medium" risk without evidence. The risk row "Latent discovery floods review surfaces with low-value proposals" at [`04_Implementation_Roadmap.md:722`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/product/04_Implementation_Roadmap.md#L722) calls out exactly the concern that makes the value rating questionable: if proposal floods are the risk, then the value is contingent on preventing floods, and preventing floods has not been demonstrated.

A saner sequence: ship digest (weeks 23-25), ship related-links and MOCs (original weeks 26-28), see whether the `_llm_metadata.<producer>` proposal pattern is bending under the weight of new producers, then decide whether a shared proposal layer is worth formalizing. The current sequence does the formalization before anything is bending.

## 6. Unstated Assumptions & Hidden Failure Modes

Unstated assumptions:

- **LLM output quality is good enough for proposal floods to be signal, not noise.** The revision assumes discovery proposals will be worth showing. Strategic memo at [`strategic_direction_memo.md:38`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/product/strategic_direction_memo.md#L38) demands the system "know what it doesn't know" — but nowhere does the proposal layer spec define calibration, an acceptance-rate floor, or a minimum confidence before a proposal surfaces. Flood risk is listed ([`04_Implementation_Roadmap.md:722`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/product/04_Implementation_Roadmap.md#L722)) but the only mitigation is "require rejection memory before scaling proposal volume" — that is circular; you cannot measure acceptance quality until proposals are flowing.
- **Operator attention is high.** Every proposal requires human decision. The revision has no "auto-accept above confidence X" path, no batch accept, no "silence this producer for 24h" toggle. Johnny is at 10-15 hours/week development time per the roadmap's reality check ([`04_Implementation_Roadmap.md:654`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/product/04_Implementation_Roadmap.md#L654)) on top of an engagement. A review surface without batch operations will be abandoned on day three.
- **Vault size stays small.** Rejection memory grows without bound. Fingerprint dimensionality is unbounded (any input change → new fingerprint). A 200-deck vault after a model upgrade could theoretically re-surface every previously rejected proposal. No retention or compaction policy is specified.
- **Tier 3 is stable.** The revision assumes `folio links`, `folio graph`, `folio entities merge`, `folio analysis init` are frozen surfaces. They just shipped on 2026-04-15 ([`2026-04-15_tier-4-graph-ops-layer.md`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/logs/2026-04-15_tier-4-graph-ops-layer.md)). There is no soak-test period baked in. The proposal revision is being merged the same day as the feature it builds on.
- **Digest and synthesize will adopt the shared proposal contract naturally.** FR-803 and FR-804 are edited to say they SHALL emit through the shared proposal layer ([`02_Product_Requirements_Document.md:987`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/product/02_Product_Requirements_Document.md#L987), [`02_Product_Requirements_Document.md:1010`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/product/02_Product_Requirements_Document.md#L1010)), but neither FR has been implemented; retrofitting a shared contract onto surfaces that do not exist is speculative.

Hidden failure modes:

- **Empty-input digest paired with silent flagged exclusion.** Daily digest exits cleanly with "nothing to digest" ([`tier4_digest_design_spec.md:111`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/specs/tier4_digest_design_spec.md#L111)) when the only candidates were flagged. The operator has no signal that content existed but was filtered out. They will believe the day was empty.
- **Rejection memory rehydration after a history rewrite.** If the library is rebased, cloned to a new machine, or rebuilt from source, rejection memory may live in a sidecar that is not copied. Revision does not say where rejection memory persists.
- **`schema_gate_result` regression over time.** FR-812 lists governed relations. If the relation schema is tightened later (e.g., adding cardinality limits), every previously-accepted proposal can become retroactively invalid. The spec says staleness fires on "relation-rule or schema-gate changes" ([`tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md:156`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/specs/tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md#L156)), which means every schema tightening mass-invalidates canonical accepted state. That is a legitimate goal but also a foot-gun.
- **"Expired or superseded" as coequal states.** The lifecycle list at [`tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md:115-116`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/specs/tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md#L115) lists both `expired` and `superseded`. But section 6 state expectations at [`tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md:129-130`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/specs/tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md#L129) treats them as "bounded queue management" together. They are not the same thing: `superseded` implies a successor exists, `expired` implies time passed. Conflating them means the CLI cannot answer "which of these rejected proposals actually have a better newer version" — a question a user will ask.

## 7. Measurability & Exit Criteria

The Acceptance Criteria in the primary spec are soft. All five items in Section 11 ([`tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md:216-224`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/specs/tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md#L216)) are documentation checks, not product checks:

- "Tier 4 docs distinguish canonical graph state from non-canonical latent discovery views" — checked by reading docs.
- "Proposal objects have one shared minimum contract across Tier 4" — checked by reading the contract.
- "Proposal lifecycle states, rejection memory, and stale invalidation are explicitly defined" — checked by reading definitions.
- "Trust-aware graph behavior applies to proposal surfacing as well as downstream graph outputs" — checked by reading the rule.
- "The spec does not commit to a custom graph database..." — checked by negation.

None of these measure whether the thing works for a user. A spec can satisfy all five with zero product value delivered. That is the definition of a non-falsifiable exit criterion.

The roadmap's Tier 4 Exit Criteria at [`04_Implementation_Roadmap.md:601-617`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/product/04_Implementation_Roadmap.md#L601) is somewhat better — it mentions "Weekly digest is used in at least one real SteerCo-prep cycle" — but the new additions ("Graph backlog and graph-health problems are inspectable without manual note-by-note inspection", "Proposal review rules cover rejection memory and stale invalidation before proposal volume scales up", etc.) are structured around the system's self-description, not the user's time or outcome. "Inspectable" is not a measurable threshold. "Before proposal volume scales up" is conditional on a future state that may never arrive.

Promotion gates for the validation workstream at [`tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md:207-212`](/Users/jonathanoh/Dev/folio.love/docs/specs/tier4_discovery_proposal_layer_spec.md#L207) include "top-ranked proposal acceptance quality is strong enough to trust review ordering" and "review burden stays manageable." Strong enough and manageable are not numbers. In a review document that demands falsifiability, this is exactly the soft language the spec should have declined.

Concrete observable exit criteria that the spec is missing:
- a cap on review queue size per week or per engagement
- a minimum proposal acceptance rate below which a producer is automatically silenced
- an observed user decision time per proposal (if it exceeds N seconds, bundle rendering is insufficient)
- a maximum percentage of flagged content in an engagement before synthesis output is considered unreliable
- an empirical validation that `folio digest` run on a week of real engagement content produces a usable artifact

Nothing in this PR forces those into view.

## 9. Verdict & Unblocking Conditions

**Request Changes.**

This revision is a docs-only proposal whose dominant effect is to lengthen Tier 4's infrastructure commitment (17.5 → 22 weeks) while shipping no additional user-visible surface, justified by a new three-layer terminology (latent / proposal / canonical) that is not load-bearing for the user. The defensible content here is approximately two pages: rejection memory, trust-aware input gating (with an override), and relation-schema validation for the high-volume relation `relates_to`. The rest is premature formalization of concepts the codebase has not yet produced.

To unblock, the proposal must change as follows. These are specific and testable:

1. **Open the spec with a user problem.** Replace Section 1 Overview with one engagement moment — e.g., Johnny reviews proposed links during SteerCo prep and the same rejected suggestion keeps reappearing, costing him N minutes per run. Name the user, the moment, the cost, and the expected post-change cost. If that problem cannot be articulated, the spec should be withdrawn.
2. **Resolve C-3.** Either remove FR-810 from this revision and defer `folio enrich diagnose` to a later slice, or remove "New CLI command families" from the Non-Goals. The two cannot both hold.
3. **Replace all documentation-check acceptance criteria with product-outcome criteria.** FR-810/811/812/813/814 each need at least one falsifiable outcome: a measurable review-queue size, an observed user decision time, a digest-usability test in real engagement context, or a minimum proposal acceptance rate. Soft criteria ("explicitly defined", "explicitly rebuildable", "strong enough") are rejected.
4. **Pin `input_fingerprint` semantics.** State exactly which inputs feed the fingerprint and which do not. Explicitly address prompt changes, model upgrades, and tokenizer differences. Without this pin, rejection memory is a fiction.
5. **Specify an `--include-flagged` override (or equivalent) for digest/synthesize/search/traversal.** The default-exclude-flagged rule cannot ship in v1 without a safety valve. If the valve is deferred, the default rule must be deferred with it.
6. **Add a review-queue bound.** Either a per-engagement cap, a per-producer silence threshold, or an auto-suppress rule when acceptance rate falls below N%. The "rejection memory before scaling proposal volume" mitigation is not a gate; it is an aspiration.
7. **Bring `relates_to` into FR-812 v1.** `relates_to` is where LLM-suggested links will concentrate. Governing `supersedes`/`impacts`/`draws_from`/`depends_on` while leaving `relates_to` ungoverned validates the small queues and ignores the big one.
8. **Shrink scope: drop FR-813 from this revision.** The latent discovery layer is naming a concept that has zero implementations. Formalize it when the first concrete producer (candidate merges from `folio entities suggest-merges`) actually starts generating non-link proposals. Keep FR-814 if you want, but cut its lifecycle states from 8 to 4 (`suggested`, `accepted`, `rejected`, `stale`) until a need for the other four is demonstrated.
9. **Remove the "shadow graph" hedge.** Either commit to the `latent discovery layer` naming or drop the term. The architecture doc cannot both define formal terminology and disavow the informal alternative in the same paragraph.
10. **Fix Mi-4.** Rename the log file or change its event type so filename and content match.

If changes 1, 3, 5, 6, and 8 are made, this becomes a credible product revision rather than an architectural framing exercise. Without them, the revision rearranges documentation without changing what ships for the user, and the roadmap grows without a corresponding outcome gain. Recommendation: request changes; do not merge.
